FAKE OFF

Do you have what it takes to contrive fictitious yet coherent details to prove the hypothetical scenarios? Can you lie your way out of a notional situation to reach for the heights?
Do you have the confidence to forge details so easily that even Sherlock Holmes can't suspect them? If yes, then Fake Off is the place for you to prove your skills!

Select any topic from the following and Fake it Off -

  • 1. Humans have evolved from wolves.
  • 2. North Korea is an exemplary democracy.
  • 3. Michael Jackson is still alive.
  • 4. Earth revolves around moon.
  • 5. Kohinoor is still in India.
  • 6. Bitcoin is the official currency of India.
  • 7. Sound travels faster than light.
  • 8. Dinosaurs are still alive.
  • 9. We inhale CO2 and exhale O2
Rules and Regulation

  • Mail your entry in the form of a report to dtu.macs@gmail.com. The subject of the e-mail should be of the format: (Fake Off entry) (topic)
  • Entries shall be accepted in PDF format only. Images are allowed in the report.
  • Word limit is 500 words. The report must contain a bibliography section to cite sources.
  • Mention your Name(s) and Contact info in the report as well as the email.
  • The deadline for the submission of entries is - 7th Feb 2017 11:59 pm
  • The top 6 teams will qualify for the final round, where each team shall be given 5 minutes to give a presentation on either the same topic or any other topic among the ones listed above. The presentation can include relevant videos.
  • Cross questions by other team members and the judges are allowed after the completion of the presentation.
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

TIC - TAC - RUN

The game of Tic - Tac - Toe was never more exciting! Testing your fitness and intelligence to the core, Tic - Tac - Run is sure to guarantee you unlimited Fun…!!!

About:

Each participant will have one placement scarf. On go, the first person from each team will run down to the tic-tac-toe board and place their scarves in one of the squares. After they place the scarf, they will race back to their line and high five the next member in line. The next guy will then place it’s scarf in an empty square. The GOAL is to have your team get 3 in a row (horizontally, vertically or diagonally). If all the 3 scarves have been placed and there is no tic-tac-toe, the next guys in line will run down and move one of their team's scarf into an open square. The game will continue in this manner. If after 4 such rounds no team wins, a tie will be declared.(1 round= All the 3 scarves have been placed on the board)

Rules:
  1. This is a team event. Each team will have 2-4 players.
  2. Each team will be given 3 items(scarves) each.
  3. The scarves must be placed properly on the board. They should not touch the lines of the squares.
  4. The player from either team reaching the board first, has to place the item first.
  5. No one is allowed to take a pause of more than 3 seconds at the board.
  6. The organising team reserves the right to change or modify any of the rules.

Wall Street Journal


About:

If you don’t find a way to make money while you sleep , you will work until you die -- Warren Buffett.
Who can better relate with this quote other than us, the millennials. Often tagged as ‘lazy’ and ‘impatient’ most of us have found our role model in Mr. Buffett and are seeking to make a career in the financial world.If you are one of them then this event is for you to show your prowess. Wall Street Journal is about predicting the market and coming out with the best Investment Strategies to win big in the Stock Market.

Topics covered:

Finance, business, stock market and of course some Mathematical aptitude.

Rounds:

Prelims and Final.

General Rules:
  1. Each team shall have two members.
  2. The organising team will discuss the rules in detail before conducting every round
  3. The Judges’ decision shall be final and binding.
Prelims:
  1. It will be a pen-paper round.
  2. 30 questions would be asked related to finance, business and stock market to the teams.
  3. Top teams with highest scores will enter the finals.
Final Round:
  1. All the teams shortlisted from the prelim will enter the finale

Relay Maths

About:

In such a fast paced world, everyone is expected to run and compete. Asymptotes 17 brings you one of its most loved events, back again. Relay Maths (who doesn’t love relay races! right?). An event which will test you on your aptitude skills, time management strategies, work ethics and most importantly your team spirit. Are you ready to be enthralled and fall in love with Maths again?

Topics covered:

MAT (Mental Ability Test) questions involving logical puzzles and mathematical riddles

Rounds:

Prelims and Presentation Round.

General Rules:
  1. This will be a team event. Each team may consist of maximum 3 members.
  2. The marking scheme and the the rules would also be explained on the spot before conducting the rounds too.
  3. The Judges’ decision shall be final and binding.
Prelims:
  1. This would be a paper - pen round.
  2. This round would consist of 30 aptitude based MCQ questions for which the teams shall be given a maximum of 30 minutes.
  3. The top 6 teams in the prelims would proceed to the final round.
Final Round:(Duration 20 minutes)
  1. Final round is inspired from Relay Race.
  2. Each team would be given a box with 15 chits corresponding to 15 different questions
  3. The teams would initially be positioned at the starting line with their boxes kept at the finishing line.
  4. As the timer starts,one member would run towards the starting line, pick up a chit and start solving it. He won’t be allowed to change the chit once opened.
  5. He can either
    • solve the question and submit it
    • pass the question
    • discard the question (the question can’t be reattempted by the team)
  6. He would then run back to the starting line,pass the baton to the next member who would do the same.
  7. The process would continue until all the questions have been answered or the time ends.
  8. If all the questions have been answered before the time ends,then the remaining time will be added to the team’s mark list.
  9. If all the questions have been answered the teams can attempt bonus questions too but they will carry negative marks. Bonus Questions can only be answered after all the 15 questions have been attempted.
  10. Its upto the teams if they want to attend the bonus questions or use the remaining time to be counted in their final mark list directly (though bonus questions would carry greater marks then the time remaining)
  11. Once a question is answered, it cannot be changed.
  12. The organising team reserves the right to modify or cancel any rules.
